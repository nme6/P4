CONSOLE GAMING

For the last few years, due to the pandemic causing a huge demand in technology (computers, smart devices, webcams, etc.), there has been a global silicon/semiconductor shortage. As time has progressed, the shortage has slowly gotten easier to manage and maintain. This is not me saying that the shortage is gone, it is still very much here and prevalent in the tech industry.

However, as the last 3 years have come and gone, prices for consoles are finally starting to drop as the market cools down and more anti-scalper measures are put into place. It can be seen as the price for consoles on third-party sites (eBay) have seen a price drop over the past few months.

As can be seen from GamingIntel, while the article is from January of this year, it still completely applies as it has been far easier in recent times to be able to grab an Xbox Series X/S, PS5, and Nintendo Switch (normal and OLED model).
