PC GAMING

For the last few years, due to the pandemic causing a huge demand in technology (computers, smart devices, webcams, etc.), there has been a global silicon/semiconductor shortage. As time has progressed, the shortage has slowly gotten easier to manage and maintain. This is not me saying that the shortage is gone, it is still very much here and prevalent in the tech industry.

However, as the last 3 years have come and gone, prices for PC parts are finally starting to drop as the market cools down and more anti-scalper measures are put into place. It can be seen as graphic cards (or GPUs) have seen a big price drop over the past few months.

As noted by Tom's Hardware, while they are using eBay GPU average pricing in their first table, this also is one of the most feasible ways to currently purchase a GPU slightly above MSRP (with current price drops). They do continue on to mention that buying from retail stores is more or less the same story as the MSRP was boosted due to scalping and is now slowly coming down as well.
